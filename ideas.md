## Idea 1: "Precision Blueprint"

**Design Movement:** Technical Brutalism meets Swiss Grid

**Core Principles:**
- Structural clarity through exposed grid lines and technical annotations
- Monochromatic base with a single crimson accent (OU Sooner red)
- Data-driven aesthetic — typography as structure, not decoration
- Asymmetric column layouts that mirror engineering schematics

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: near-white (#F8F7F4) — warm paper tone, not cold white
- Primary text: deep charcoal (#1A1A2E)
- Accent: OU Crimson (#841617) — used sparingly for headings and borders
- Secondary: steel blue (#2D5FA0) — for links and technical callouts

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Off-center hero with PI photo anchored left, text flowing right at 60/40 split
- Navigation: left-rail fixed sidebar on desktop, collapsible on mobile
- Research cards in a masonry-style grid with visible border lines

**Signature Elements:**
- Thin horizontal rule lines between sections (1px crimson)
- Monospaced font for labels and metadata (e.g., "Research Focus / 01")
- Trajectory arc SVG decorations in section backgrounds

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover reveals: research cards expand to show abstract on hover
- Smooth section transitions with subtle slide-in from left

**Animation:**
- Entrance: staggered fade-up for list items (50ms delay between items)
- Nav: underline slides in from left on hover
- Cards: slight translateY(-4px) + shadow deepening on hover

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (serif) — for lab name and section headers
- Body: Source Sans Pro — for all body text
- Mono: JetBrains Mono — for labels, metadata, publication numbers
</text>

## Idea 2: "Deep Space Control" (SELECTED)

**Design Movement:** Scientific Dark Academia meets Aerospace Minimalism

**Core Principles:**
- Deep navy/slate backgrounds evoking aerospace and control theory
- Clean geometric layouts with mathematical precision
- OU Crimson as the sole warm accent against cool tones
- Content-first hierarchy — no decorative clutter, every element serves information

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: deep navy (#0D1B2A) — evokes aerospace, control systems, night sky
- Surface: dark slate (#1B2A3B) — for cards and panels
- Primary accent: OU Crimson (#841617) — for active states, headings, highlights
- Secondary accent: electric blue (#4A9EFF) — for links and interactive elements
- Text: off-white (#E8EDF2) — warm enough to avoid harshness

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Full-width hero with animated particle/trajectory background
- Horizontal top navigation with logo left, links right
- Alternating left/right content blocks for research sections
- Two-column team layout with photo + bio cards

**Signature Elements:**
- Thin glowing lines (1px, 20% opacity blue) as section dividers
- Subtle grid dot pattern as background texture on hero
- Crimson left-border accent on blockquotes and featured publications

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Cards lift with a glow effect on hover
- Navigation links have an underline that grows from center outward
- Smooth scroll between sections

**Animation:**
- Hero: fade-in with slight scale from 0.98 to 1.0
- Section entries: slide-up with opacity transition (0.4s ease-out)
- Cards: box-shadow transition from subtle to prominent on hover

**Typography System:**
- Display: Space Grotesk (geometric sans) — for lab name and section headers
- Body: Inter — for readable body text
- Accent: IBM Plex Mono — for publication metadata and labels

## Selected Design: Idea 2 — "Deep Space Control"

Rationale: The aerospace/control systems research theme maps naturally to a deep navy palette. The dark background with crimson and electric blue accents creates a distinctive, professional identity that stands apart from generic academic sites while honoring OU's brand colors. The geometric precision of Space Grotesk reinforces the mathematical rigor of the research.
